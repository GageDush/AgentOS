# Platform Template Motion Reference Pack

## Template Read

Your `Platform` landing page already has a strong baseline:

- dark charcoal foundation
- warm orange accent
- particle-wave hero canvas
- masked hover button treatment
- animated stat counters
- character-based intro text reveal
- product grid with floating 3D assets

That means the best additions are not "more effects everywhere."
The right move is to layer in a few high-quality interactions that deepen the existing tone:

- stronger landing animation
- smoother section-to-section scroll reveals
- one or two proximity effects
- a tasteful loader
- richer product-card motion

## Best-Fit Motion References

### 1. Hero landing choreography

Best source:

- Framer animation examples: [framer.com/blog/website-animation-examples](https://www.framer.com/blog/website-animation-examples/)

Why it fits:

- Your template already opens with a canvas-backed hero.
- Framer’s best examples lean on staged entrances, subtle background motion, and layered copy reveals instead of a giant one-shot gimmick.

What to borrow:

- headline lines entering in sequence
- CTA and proof stats fading in a beat later
- background motion already alive before text settles
- one secondary object drifting or rotating very slowly in the hero

### 2. Scroll reveal for copy blocks

Best source:

- React Bits Scroll Reveal: [reactbits.dev/text-animations/scroll-reveal](https://reactbits.dev/text-animations/scroll-reveal)

Why it fits:

- Your intro section already hints at text-based reveal behavior.
- This complements your premium SaaS tone without turning every section into a circus.

What to use it for:

- intro statement
- section openers
- feature headlines
- testimonial pull quotes

Avoid:

- applying it to long paragraphs
- revealing every single text block on the page

### 3. Proximity-based headline or nav treatment

Best source:

- React Bits Variable Proximity: [reactbits.dev/text-animations/variable-proximity](https://reactbits.dev/text-animations/variable-proximity)

Why it fits:

- You specifically like proximity motion.
- This effect feels modern and playful when used once or twice, especially with your orange-on-dark palette.

Best placements:

- hero eyebrow or slogan
- nav labels on desktop
- feature category labels
- footer callout text

Do not use it on:

- body copy
- pricing tables
- forms

### 4. Scroll-velocity accent band

Best source:

- React Bits Scroll Velocity: [reactbits.dev/text-animations/scroll-velocity](https://reactbits.dev/text-animations/scroll-velocity)

Why it fits:

- This gives you fun scrolling energy without needing a heavy 3D scene.
- It works well as a divider between hero and content or between product and testimonial sections.

Best placements:

- a slim marquee-style transition band
- "automation / scaling / orchestration" style keyword strip
- client or capability ticker

### 5. Staggered section entrances

Best source:

- React Bits Animated Content: [reactbits.dev/animations/animated-content](https://reactbits.dev/animations/animated-content)

Why it fits:

- This is the safest way to make the site feel more alive overall.
- It pairs naturally with cards, feature grids, logos, and FAQ rows.

Best placements:

- feature cards
- work / product grid cards
- testimonial blocks
- logo rows
- FAQ items

### 6. Premium landing-page benchmark set

Best sources:

- Framer landing page marketplace: [framer.com/marketplace/templates/category/landing-page/](https://www.framer.com/marketplace/templates/category/landing-page/)
- Framer landing page gallery: [framer.com/gallery/categories/landing-page](https://www.framer.com/gallery/categories/landing-page)

Why they fit:

- These are the closest references to your current template DNA.
- They help you benchmark pacing, spacing, hero density, product cards, and CTA rhythm.

What to study:

- how many animated elements are active at once
- how product cards move on hover
- how proof sections transition in
- how nav bars change after scroll

### 7. Scroll ideas for one standout section

Best sources:

- Codrops Creative Hub: [tympanus.net/codrops/hub/](https://tympanus.net/codrops/hub/)
- Codrops CSS scroll-driven article: [tympanus.net/codrops/all-articles/page/25/?ref=driverlayer.com](https://tympanus.net/codrops/all-articles/page/25/?ref=driverlayer.com)
- Codrops WebGL scroll article: [tympanus.net/codrops/2020/10/19/implementing-webgl-powered-scroll-animations/](https://tympanus.net/codrops/2020/10/19/implementing-webgl-powered-scroll-animations/)

Why they fit:

- Codrops is where to go when you want one section to feel special.
- For this template, that should be one section only, probably the product/work block.

Good borrow ideas:

- horizontal parallax gallery
- image planes with slight depth motion
- sticky section with layered text and media
- subtle scroll-linked transforms

Avoid:

- making the whole page a scroll experiment
- WebGL in multiple sections

## Components To Add

### Highest value

- animated hero loading sequence
- sticky / morphing nav on scroll
- staggered feature-card entrances
- product-card hover elevation plus media drift
- proximity text on one headline or nav surface
- slim scroll-velocity divider
- tasteful loader

### Good second wave

- animated logo cloud
- FAQ expand/collapse with eased height and opacity
- testimonial cards with parallax drift
- CTA strip with background motion

### Likely too much for this template

- huge liquid distortion scenes
- constant cursor trails
- multiple different parallax systems
- loud page-transition wipes
- loading screen longer than about 1.2s

## Recommended Motion Plan

If I were tuning this exact template, I would do:

1. Hero load:
   particle wave already moving, then headline, then CTA, then stats

2. Intro:
   keep the text reveal, but tighten timing and make it feel more deliberate

3. Work/Product grid:
   add staggered card reveal and more satisfying hover motion

4. Between sections:
   add one scroll-velocity band or one horizontal parallax break

5. Nav:
   subtle scroll-state change plus maybe a proximity treatment on desktop

6. Loader:
   one short geometric loader, then straight into the hero

## The Vibe To Preserve

The current template reads as:

- premium
- structured
- slightly futuristic
- product-focused

So the motion should feel:

- precise
- warm
- intentional
- smooth

Not:

- chaotic
- hyper-neon
- game-like
- overengineered

- optional Spline object only if it stays lightweight
